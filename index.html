About Me

I am a third-year student at the University of Alberta studying Computing Science Specialization. My primary interest as of now is software development in both front-end and back-end.

I received an NSERC Undergraduate Research Award to help conduct a SARS-CoV-2 study during the Summer of 2021. I assisted in performing various experiments for mutation analyses and time-series predictions. Further, I contributed in manuscript writing and editing. Our team established the correlation of mutations in the N-terminal domain (NTD), receptor-binding domain (RBD), and other vital regions in the spike protein using identified mutation pairs with high rates of co-occurrence and re-occurrence. We determined functional mutations using their biological implications through scientific reading. We also used machine learning algorithms to forecast spike mutations of Covid-19 to predict its future spread.

In addition to the above, I have significant experience in both collaborative and individual settings by working in a non-industry job for less than three years. I also have a year of formal tutoring experience with Ada's Team for two introductory computing courses. The courses introduce Computing Science students to functional programming, data structures, and algorithms in Python.

Currently, I am an Senior Peer Advisor at the University of Alberta Students' Union, Infolink. In this role, I provide academic and campus guidance through email, in-person, and telephone correspondence.
